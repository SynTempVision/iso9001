# 5.3 Organizational Roles, Responsibilities, and Authorities

Document ID: SOP-5.3  
Version: 1.0  
Owner: CEO  
Approved By: CEO  
Effective Date: 2026-03-01  

---

## Purpose

To define organizational roles, responsibilities, and authorities necessary for the effective implementation and maintenance of the Quality Management System (QMS).

---

## Top Management

Top Management is responsible for:

- Overall effectiveness of the Quality Management System
- Establishing and approving the Quality Policy
- Establishing Quality Objectives
- Ensuring adequate resources are available
- Participating in Management Review
- Ensuring customer and regulatory requirements are met

---

## Quality Manager (or Quality Lead)

The Quality Manager is responsible for:

- Maintaining QMS documentation
- Ensuring documents are controlled and current
- Coordinating internal audits
- Monitoring nonconformances and corrective actions
- Reporting QMS performance to Top Management

---

## Operations / Production Personnel

Operations personnel are responsible for:

- Following work order requirements
- Performing manufacturing and assembly activities as defined
- Identifying and reporting nonconforming outputs
- Maintaining required production records

---

## Inspection / Verification Personnel

Inspection personnel are responsible for:

- Performing verification, testing, or calibration activities
- Confirming products meet acceptance criteria
- Approving products for release
- Preventing release of nonconforming products

---

## All Employees

All personnel are responsible for:

- Following established procedures
- Maintaining quality awareness
- Reporting issues or risks that may affect product conformity

---

## Communication

Roles and responsibilities are communicated through:

- This documented procedure
- Training and onboarding
- Management meetings
- Organizational charts (if applicable)

---

## Review

Roles and responsibilities are reviewed during Management Review and updated as necessary.
